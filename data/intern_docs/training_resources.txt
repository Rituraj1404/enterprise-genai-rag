Intern Training Resources

This document lists the learning resources and internal tools available to interns during their tenure.

Internal Tools
- Jira: used for sprint planning and task tracking. Every intern is added to their team's board in the first week.
- Confluence: internal documentation wiki for engineering processes, architecture diagrams, and meeting notes.
- Slack: primary communication tool. Key channels include #general, #engineering, #random, and #it-helpdesk.

Learning Plan
Interns are expected to complete an internal "Bootcamp" track covering company codebases, coding standards, and the deployment pipeline within the first 2 weeks. The Bootcamp is self-paced but mentors will check progress weekly.

Weekly Check-ins
Every Friday, interns have a 30-minute sync with their mentor to discuss progress, blockers, and learning goals for the following week.

Performance Reviews
Interns receive informal feedback every 2 weeks and a formal written review at the 3-month and 6-month marks. These reviews form the basis for any full-time conversion decisions.

Internal Communication Etiquette
Use threads in Slack for detailed discussions to keep channels readable. Avoid direct messages for work-related decisions; prefer team channels for visibility.
