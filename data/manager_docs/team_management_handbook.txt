Manager Handbook: Team Management Practices

This document outlines core responsibilities and practices for engineering managers.

Team Structure
Each manager typically oversees a team of 5-8 engineers, including a mix of senior, mid-level, and intern roles. Managers report into a Director, who reports into VP Engineering.

One-on-Ones
Managers are expected to hold biweekly 1:1 meetings with each direct report. Topics should include career growth, current project blockers, and general well-being check-ins.

Performance Reviews
Managers conduct formal performance reviews twice a year (mid-year and annual). Reviews should be based on documented examples of work, peer feedback, and goal completion against the OKRs set at the start of the cycle.

Hiring and Interviews
Managers participate in the hiring loop for their team's open positions, typically conducting the technical deep-dive round and the final hiring decision meeting alongside HR.

Budget and Headcount Planning
Each quarter, managers submit headcount requests and budget justifications to their Director. This includes intern-to-fulltime conversion proposals, which require performance review documentation.

Escalation Process
If a team member raises a conduct or HR issue, managers must escalate to HR within 24 hours and should not attempt to resolve serious matters informally.
